Chromatose

The works in this series, we move away from radial symmetry in favor of linear, sweeping trajectories that mimic the slow , powerful drift of continental plates and the flow of molten magma. The viewer witnesses a rupture that cuts through dense textures, echoing the scars found in a parched earth.

Radiant Expansions

Through a technical mastery of radial composition, the works utilize a powerful central nucleus to drow the viewer's eye into a singular point of origin. The focal point acts as an anchor for the surrounding chaos. where streaks of electric blue, violets, and neon orange vibrate against the void of deep blacks and iridescent shadows.

The Elemental Flux

Each work in the collection is a frozen moment of transition. Using high-pigment acrylics and varied pouring techniques, the artist mimics the physical laws of gravity, buoyancy, and surface tension. The resulting canvases do not merely depict nature; they embody the same chaotic forces that shape river deltas, volcanic strata, and stellar nebulae.

About the Artist

Art has been my shadow since I could walk, but it took a detour through 35 years of hairdressing to find its true form. Based in Austin, TX, I'm an experimentalist at heart who swapped hair dye for high-flow acrylics.

I use my deep-rooted knowledge of color theory to orchestrate collisions between chemistry and creativity. The result? Macro-organic patterns that celebrate the beauty of the unpredictable. I don't paint static images; I capture movement suspended in time.

Working with professional-grade acrylics and innovative pouring techniques, Chris creates vibrant, one-of-a-kind artworks that capture movement and emotion in abstract form. Each series represents a unique exploration of theme, palette, and methodology.
